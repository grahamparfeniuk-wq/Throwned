# Emotional Engine

This document is the behavioral thesis for Throned.

If mechanics docs explain what happens, this explains why people care.

## Competitive Identity Thesis

Throned does not optimize for passive attention loops.
It optimizes for survival under direct comparison.

Users attach when they:
- defend contenders
- discover contenders early
- participate in ascension and dethroning
- follow rivalry and status movement across time

The emotional center is relational and consequential: someone rises, someone falls.

## Psychological Pillars

| Pillar | Meaning in Throned |
|--------|--------------------|
| Tribal identity | "My arena, my lane, my contender." |
| Discovery ownership | Emotional stake from early support. |
| Rivalry continuity | Repeat conflict builds mythology. |
| Status movement | Climb, defense, slippage, redemption remain legible. |
| Participatory ascension | Users feel they influenced who rose. |
| Belonging | Affiliation survives losses; loyalty matters. |
| Aspiration | Contenders represent identity and ambition. |

## Design Constraints

- Avoid feed psychology and dashboard framing.
- Keep gestures primary and chrome minimal.
- Keep overlays in broadcast language, not utility language.
- Keep narrative sparse but meaningful.
- Keep hierarchy movement understandable and earned.

## Product Bug Signals

Treat these as doctrine regressions:
- passive browsing replacing decision moments
- engagement bait without competitive consequence
- gamified noise that cheapens contenders
- opaque ranking shifts that feel unearned

## Moderation Philosophy Link

Moderation should protect:
- participant safety
- competition legitimacy
- emotional integrity of the arena

Moderation should remove manipulation and abuse that hollow competition into spectacle.
